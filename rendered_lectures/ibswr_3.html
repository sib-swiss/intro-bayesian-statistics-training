Monte Carlo approximation

In general, these integrals are expectations

E[g(x)]=g(x)p(x)dxE[g(x)]=g(x)p(x)dx

If we can sample proportionally to p(x)p(x)

  • can use Monte Carlo approximation

E[g(x)]1Mi=1Mg(xi)E[g(x)]1Mi=1Mg(xi)

  • for MM random samples xixi, sampled p(x)p(x)

Accuracy typically scales like 1M1M for any dimension

  • number of bins for numerical integration grows exponentially in the dimension

Lies behind lots of Bayesian analyses

Question: how do we get samples from arbitrary distributions?

MCMC

Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) Metropolis, Rosenbluth2, Teller2 (1953); Hastings (1970)

To sample proportionally to any function p(x)p(x):

[1.] From current position xx, propose a new position xx

x=x+,q(d)x=x+,q(d)

  • with a symmetric distribution qq(d), eg a normal (can modify algorithm for asymmetric too)

[2.] Accept xx with probability

min(1,p(x)p(x))min(1,p(x)p(x))

  • and move current position to xx
  • otherwise stay at xx

[3.] Repeat, thats all!

  • (though it may take many iterations to converge)

HMC

Hamiltonian Monte Carlo (HMC) Duane, Kennedy, Pendleton and Roweth (1987); Neal (1995, 2011)

To sample proportionally to any function p(x)p(x): add a new dimension !

Make Gaussian, and define the negative log-likelihood as an energy surface

H(x,)=log{p(x)}+22H(x,)=log{p(x)}+22

[1.] From current position xx, sample a Gaussian and propose a new pair (x,)(x,) by propagating (numerically) under the (energy-preserving) Hamiltonian dynamics: dxdt=H,ddt=Hxdxdt=H,ddt=Hx

[2.] Accept xx with probability

min(1,exp{H(x,)H(x,)})1min(1,exp{H(x,)H(x,)})1

  • and move current position to xx
  • otherwise stay at xx

[3.] Repeat, thats all!
